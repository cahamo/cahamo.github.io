Electro-Tongue Drum Project

Introduction

This is a project to build an electronic emulation of a Tongue Drum, also known as the tank drum or a hank drum.

I've described elsewhere what my motivation is to build this project. See my post “My 1st Project - Say Hello to the ‘Electro Tongue Drum’’ in my blog for more information.

Project Brief

The main features of the electro-tongue drum are:

  • The shape of the instrument will echo that of the acoustic instrument, but will not copy it slavishly. Think of the difference between an acoustic and electric guitar. This intention is that the instrument will be circular or polygonal in cross section, but will be relatively flat. It is not desirable to introduce a large chamber that could amplify the percusive sound of fingers hitting keys.
  • The instrument will be operated by means of touch sensitve keys, laid out in a similar pattern to a tongue drum. This is advisable to make the transition between the acoustic instrument and this electronic version as seemless as possible. IUnfortunatley tongue drums come with varying numbers of keys, so a decision needs to be taken how many the project instrument will have.
  • There should be two modes of operation:
    1. Audio mode where the instrument produces sound when a key is pressed. The sound may be reproduced via an internal speaker and/or it may be output via a line level audio output or to headphones. A decision is required as to whether the sound is synthesised or is made by triggering samples of an aoustic instrument.
    2. MIDI mode where the instrument output MIDI note on and off messages is response to key hits. Such messages will be sent to suitable software that can use the notes to drive MIDI instruments. In particular the notes should be compatible with tongue drum virtual instruments.
  • The instrument should be capabable of stand alone operation which implies at least an option for battery power.

Progress

At present this is a very new project and I am very inexperienced, having just begun to learn electronics in April of 2021.

The project is very much in the experimental phase. Having determined that the touch sensitive keys are to use capacitive sensing, early experiments are being conducted to learn about suitable material and software.
